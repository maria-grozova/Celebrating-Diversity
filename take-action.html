How do I know when to act?

It all starts with recognising the signs of discrimination. Some of the things to look out for are if you or others are:

  • being excluded
  • being unable to express themselves as they would wish to
  • not being given responsibility for tasks they are qualified to carry out
  • having their opinions discounted
  • being subject to inappropriate remarks
  • being the target of offensive jokes
  • being subject to violence

What do I do?

Remember, if someone is in immediate danger you should call 999 or your local emergency services number

Your action will depend on your own view of what's happened and/or what the person who's being discriminated against would like to happen next.
  1. Offer/seek support
  2. Report the harrassment to employer/organisation
  3. Get additional guidance, for example contact the Equality Advisory and Support Service (EASS)
